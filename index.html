Dolly Parton

Singer, Songwriter, Humanitarian

Dolly Parton was born on January 19, 1946, in a one-room cabin in Pittman Center, Tennessee. She first appeared at the Grand Ole Opry at age 13 and moved to Nashville after graduating high school to pursue a career in music. Over the course of her career, Dolly Parton has achieved more than 100 million records sold, 25 RIAA certified gold, platinum, and multi-platinum awards, 25 #1 songs on the Billboard Country Charts, 41 top 10 country albums, 110 charted singles, 11 Grammy Awards and 50 Grammy Award nominations, two stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, the National Medal of Arts, the Kennedy Center Honors, and the Library of Congress Living Legend Medal. She has been inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame and has also received at least one nomination from the Emmy Awards, Grammy Awards, Acadamy Awards, and Tony Awards.
